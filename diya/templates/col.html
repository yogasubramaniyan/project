Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1 . Is nursing a good career choice?

Nursing has always been considered a rewarding career. It deals with the most significant aspect of the healthcare industry – patient care. A nurse is a trained professional who provides medical assistance in the various domains of the healthcare industry, making it one of the noblest professions.

How well an individual fits into this role depends on their interest and dedication because nursing, just like other healthcare professions, requires long, tireless hours and dedication. It also requires the commitment to serve with great patience.

Apart from this, nursing has good opportunities in India and abroad, in both – the private and government sectors.

2 . Which Nursing Course is the best?

One can apply for a Nursing course after having completed his 12th or equivalent.

On completion of his 12th with science subjects (Physics, Chemistry & Biology) can pursue BSc Nursing.

On completion of his 12th with other subjects (arts and commerce) can pursue General Nursing and Midwifery.

General Nursing & Midwifery: :
  1. BSc Nursing is a four-year graduation course in Nursing
  2. Eligibility: for science students only.
  3. Extensive academic scope.
  4. Students learn the aspects of assessment, planning, implementation and evaluation of patient care.
  5. Requires practical exposure.
  6. A four-year program with 24 weeks internship.

BSc Nursing:
  1. A three-year diploma course in Nursing.
  2. Eligibility: Across all streams (Science, Commerce & Arts)
  3. Limited academic scope.
  4. Students learn emergency response, compassion, individual care etc.
  5. Theoretical approach.
  6. A three-year program with 6 months internship.

6 . What is the Nursing foundation?

The Nursing Foundation encompasses autonomous and collaborative care of individuals of all age groups, families, groups and communities, sick or healthy and in all sorts of settings and environments. It includes the promotion of health, prevention of illness and the care of sick, disabled and dying people.

8. What is the salary of a government nurse?

The salary of an entry-level staff nurse in a government hospital is in the range of Rs 30,000 – 40,000/-. The pay increases with experience.

9 . What are the ranks of nurses in India?

The ranks of the nurses are as follows:
  • Nursing Superintendent
  • Nursing Supervisor
  • Head Nurse
  • Staff Nurse

10 . What are the ranks of nurses in United Kingdom?

The ranks of the nurses are as follows:
  • Band VIII Matron
  • Band VII Co-ordinator/Manager
  • Band VI Team Leader
  • Band V Staff Nurse

11 . How does one select the best nursing college in Bangalore?

If you’re looking for the top nursing colleges in Bangalore, then the first and foremost thing you need to keep in mind is the approvals and accreditations from:

  • INC – Indian Nursing Council
  • KNC – Karnataka Nursing Council
  • RGUHS – Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences.

Some of the colleges are approved by the INC – Indian Nursing Council and some colleges are approved by the KNC – Karnataka Nursing Council. It’s advisable to choose colleges approved by the Indian Nursing Council and the Karnataka Nursing Council and affiliated with the Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences (RGUHS). (A lot of colleges and consultants may not convey this to you.) This makes it easier to get approvals for student loans and future placements in the healthcare sector. Apart from this, a lot of other factors weigh in too:

  • The college should have a good state of the art infrastructure.
  • A well furnished and up-to-date library.
  • A well-equipped laboratory.
  • Qualified and well-trained faculties.
  • Clinical Experience.
  • Patient Exposure.
  • Tie-ups with hospitals and other healthcare firms.
  • Updated with the latest equipment and technology.

Diya College and School of Nursing, Bangalore, is approved by the Indian Nursing Council and the Karnataka Nursing Council. Also, it is affiliated with the Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences.
